## Why Spring Is Perfect for Family Photos

After six months of Edmonton winter, spring feels like the city takes a deep breath and wakes up. The snow melts. The grass turns green almost overnight. And the trees begin to bloom in ways that transform every park into a natural studio. As a [family photographer in Edmonton](https://mhphoto.ca/photography/family/), I can tell you that spring is one of the most beautiful — and most underrated — seasons for family portraits.

Most families think of fall for their annual photos. The golden leaves, the warm tones, the cozy outfits. And fall is beautiful, absolutely. But spring offers something fall cannot: freshness. The colours are lighter. The greens are brighter. The blossoms create a softness in the background that feels gentle and hopeful. It is the season of new beginnings, which makes it especially meaningful for families welcoming a new baby, celebrating a milestone, or simply wanting to document this chapter of their lives.

There is also a practical advantage. Spring sessions are less in demand than fall, which means more flexibility with scheduling. You are more likely to get your preferred date and time. And the locations are less crowded — no competing with fifteen other families trying to photograph at the same tree.

## The Best Time to Book Spring Sessions

Timing a spring session in Edmonton requires some knowledge of the city's natural rhythms. Edmonton's spring is not like spring in Vancouver or Toronto. It arrives later and moves faster. The entire blossom window can open and close in three weeks if the weather cooperates.

Here is my recommended timeline:

### Late April to Early May: Early Green

The snow is gone and the first green shoots are appearing. Trees are budding but not yet in full leaf. The light is soft and the grass is fresh. This is a great window for families who want a clean, minimalist look — green backgrounds without the visual complexity of flowers and blossoms. The river valley trails are particularly beautiful during this period because the underbrush has not grown in yet, creating open, airy compositions.

### Mid-May to Early June: Peak Blossom

This is the golden window. Edmonton's crabapple trees, lilac bushes, and cherry blossoms are all in bloom. The city literally transforms. Streets that looked bare two weeks ago are now canopied in pink and white petals. This is the most popular time for spring sessions, and for good reason — the blossoms create a romantic, dreamy backdrop that is impossible to replicate any other time of year.

I recommend booking your session for the last week of May or the first week of June. This catches the peak of the lilac season, which is Edmonton's most iconic spring bloom. The scent alone is worth the visit, but the photography opportunities are extraordinary — dense clusters of purple and white flowers that create depth and colour in every frame.


### Mid-June: Full Canopy

By mid-June, the blossoms have mostly dropped but the trees are in full leaf. The canopy is lush and green, creating beautiful dappled light. This is an excellent time for sessions under large trees — the overhead leaves act as a natural diffuser, softening the sunlight and creating even, flattering illumination on faces. It is also warmer, which means more comfortable sessions with young children who might not enjoy sitting still in cool weather.

Photographer Tip

The lilac season in Edmonton is incredibly short — often just 10 to 14 days. If you want lilac blooms in your photos, book your session for the last week of May and be flexible. I monitor the blooms daily during this period and will text you when they peak so we can schedule accordingly.

## What to Wear for Spring Family Photos

Outfit coordination is one of the most common questions I get from families. And spring offers some of the best colour palette options of any season. The key is to complement the environment without competing with it.

### Colours That Work Beautifully

Soft pastels are spring's best friend. Think dusty rose, sage green, light lavender, soft blue, and cream. These tones harmonize with the fresh greens and blossoms in the background without clashing. For families with young children, lighter colours also photograph well against grass and flowers, keeping the overall feel of the images light and airy.

Earthy neutrals also work wonderfully. Tan, beige, olive, and soft brown create a grounded, timeless look. Mix these with one or two accent colours — a child in a soft yellow dress, a partner in a dusty blue shirt — and you have a palette that looks coordinated without looking like everyone shopped at the same store on the same day.

### What to Avoid

- **Bright neon colours:** They pull focus away from faces and clash with natural backgrounds
- **All black:** Creates a heavy visual weight that fights against the lightness of spring
- **Busy logos or graphics:** Distract from faces and date the photos quickly
- **Matchy-matchy identical outfits:** Coordinate, do not match — variety in the same palette looks more natural

Photographer Tip

Layers are your spring secret weapon. A light cardigan, a linen jacket, a soft scarf — these add visual interest and texture to photos. They also help during Edmonton springs when the temperature can swing 10 degrees in an hour. Comfortable families make for better photos, always.

## Photographing Kids in Spring

Spring sessions with kids have a special advantage: the outdoors gives children room to move. And movement is where the magic happens. A two-year-old who would scream in a studio will happily toddle through a field of dandelions for twenty minutes. A five-year-old who refuses to smile on command will break into the most genuine grin when they discover a puddle or a bug.

My approach with children in spring sessions is simple: let them explore. I give gentle guidance to the parents — stand here, face this direction, hold hands — and then I let the kids lead. The best family photos are not the ones where everyone is staring at the camera with forced smiles. They are the ones where Dad is swinging a toddler through the air, Mom is laughing because the baby just sneezed, and the older sibling is pointing at a bird in the tree.

Spring also offers natural props. Dandelions are every child photographer's best friend. Hand a three-year-old a dandelion puff and tell them to blow. The concentration on their face as they take that big breath, followed by the explosion of seeds floating through the air — it creates images that parents frame immediately. No posing required. No forced smile needed. Just a child being a child in a beautiful setting.

## Weather and Backup Plans

Edmonton spring weather is famously unpredictable. I have shot sessions in 25-degree sunshine and sessions in 5-degree drizzle — sometimes in the same week. Having a backup plan is not optional. It is essential.

Here is how I handle weather for spring sessions:

- **Light overcast:** This is actually ideal. Overcast skies act as a giant natural softbox, creating even, flattering light with no harsh shadows. I never reschedule for overcast days — they often produce the best photos.
- **Light rain:** If it is a gentle drizzle, we can still shoot. Umbrellas become props. Rain on flower petals looks beautiful in close-ups. And the post-rain light — when the clouds start to break — is some of the most gorgeous light you will ever see.
- **Heavy rain or storms:** We reschedule. Safety comes first, always. I build flexibility into my spring schedule specifically for weather delays. Most sessions can be rescheduled within 48 hours.
- **Cold snap:** Edmonton springs can surprise you with near-freezing days even in late May. I recommend layering and bringing a warm blanket to keep kids comfortable between setups.

Photographer Tip

Download a weather app that shows hourly forecasts. Edmonton weather can change dramatically within a few hours. A rainy morning might clear into a stunning evening. I check conditions three times on the day of a session — morning, afternoon, and one hour before we start — and adjust timing if needed.

## Working with Spring Light

Spring light in Edmonton is uniquely beautiful. The sun sits lower in the sky than summer, creating longer golden hours and softer shadows. The days are getting longer — by late May, Edmonton gets nearly 17 hours of daylight — which means more scheduling flexibility for sessions.

My preferred time for spring family sessions is the last two hours before sunset. In late May, that means starting around 7:00 PM and shooting until about 8:30 PM. The light during this window is warm, directional, and incredibly flattering. It wraps around faces, creates a natural glow in hair, and paints the landscape in gold.

For families with very young children who cannot stay up that late, I offer morning sessions starting around 8:00 AM. The morning light in spring is cooler and softer — less golden but equally beautiful. It creates a fresh, clean look in photos that pairs perfectly with the spring theme.
