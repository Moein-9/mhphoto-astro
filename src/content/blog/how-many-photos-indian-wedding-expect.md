## Why Photo Count Matters for Indian Weddings

One of the first questions I hear from couples planning an [Indian wedding](https://mhphoto.ca/photography/indian-weddings/) is simple and completely fair: how many photos will we actually get? It is not a shallow question. It comes from a genuine place of wanting to make sure every ritual, every outfit change, every emotional moment, and every family member who flew in from across the world is properly documented.

Indian weddings are unlike any other celebration on the planet. They are longer. They are more complex. They involve more events, more people, and more cultural significance per moment than most Western weddings. A typical Western wedding might have one ceremony and one reception. An Indian wedding might include a mehndi, a sangeet, a haldi, a baraat, the actual ceremony, and a reception — each one with its own wardrobe, decor, rituals, and emotional weight.

So when someone asks me "how many photos should I expect," my answer always starts the same way: it depends on how many days your celebration spans. However, I can give you very specific ranges based on years of experience photographing Indian weddings right here in Edmonton.

## Photo Expectations for a Single-Day Indian Wedding

Not every Indian wedding spans multiple days. Many couples in Edmonton — especially those blending cultures or working within tighter timelines — condense their celebration into a single day. And that is completely beautiful. A single-day Indian wedding still has more going on than most multi-day Western weddings.

For a typical single-day Indian wedding with a ceremony and reception, here is what you can expect from a professional photographer who knows the culture.

### Getting Ready: 100–200 Photos

The getting-ready phase at an Indian wedding is an event in itself. The bride's preparation alone can take two to three hours. There is the lehenga or saree draping, the jewellery — which often includes multiple sets — the dupatta pinning, the mehndi touch-ups, and the emotional moments with family. The groom's preparation includes the sehra tying, the turban styling, and the sword or kirpan presentation in Sikh families. Every single detail matters.

### Ceremony: 300–500 Photos

Whether it is a Hindu, Sikh, Muslim, or fusion ceremony, the ritual portion of an Indian wedding is dense with photographable moments. The pheras, the jaimala, the vidaai, the kanyadaan — each one deserves thorough coverage. I typically shoot between 1,500 and 2,500 frames during the ceremony alone. After culling and editing, couples receive 300 to 500 of the very best.


### Reception: 300–400 Photos

The reception brings its own energy. The grand entrance, the first dance, the toasts, the family dances, the food, the decor, the candid moments on the dance floor. Indian receptions are vibrant and colourful, and they deserve thorough documentation. Expect 300 to 400 edited photos from a well-covered reception.

### Portraits: 50–100 Photos

Couple portraits, family formals, and bridal party shots typically add another 50 to 100 images to your final gallery. I always recommend building in at least 30 minutes specifically for couple portraits. Even 20 minutes in beautiful light can produce 40 to 60 stunning images.

Pro Tip

If you are having a single-day wedding, talk to your photographer about the timeline well in advance. Indian ceremonies often run longer than planned — which is beautiful and normal. Building in buffer time ensures nothing feels rushed and no moments are missed.

## What Affects Your Final Photo Count

Several factors influence how many edited photos you receive. Understanding these helps you set realistic expectations and communicate clearly with your photographer.

### Number of Events

This is the biggest factor. A two-event wedding produces roughly half the photos of a four-event wedding. Simple math. If you are booking a [multi-day package](/multiday-packages/), each event adds its own layer to the gallery.

### Guest Count

Larger weddings naturally generate more candid moments. A 500-guest Indian wedding produces more photo opportunities than a 100-guest celebration simply because there are more interactions, more reactions, and more stories unfolding simultaneously. I often shoot with a second photographer at larger celebrations to ensure full coverage.

### Hours of Coverage

More hours means more moments captured. An eight-hour coverage day produces significantly more than a five-hour day. For Indian weddings, I always recommend full-day coverage because the celebrations rarely fit into neat time blocks.

### Photographer's Editing Style

Some photographers deliver every decent frame. Others — myself included — curate tightly. I would rather deliver 1,000 exceptional images than 3,000 mediocre ones. Every photo in your gallery should make you feel something. If it does not, it should not be there.

## Editing and Gallery Delivery Timelines

Here is something many couples do not think about until after the wedding: editing takes time. Serious, skilled time. And the more photos in your gallery, the longer the editing process.

For a single-day Indian wedding with 800 to 1,200 delivered images, editing typically takes four to six weeks. For a multi-day celebration with 2,000 to 4,000 images, expect six to ten weeks. During peak wedding season — June through September in Edmonton — timelines can stretch slightly longer.

What happens during editing? Every image is individually colour-corrected, exposure-adjusted, and refined. Skin tones are carefully balanced. Distracting background elements are removed where possible. The overall mood and warmth of each image is crafted to match the feeling of the moment. For Indian weddings specifically, I pay special attention to the richness of fabrics, the sparkle of jewellery, and the warmth of skin tones against colourful backdrops.

Pro Tip

Ask your photographer about sneak peeks. Most professional wedding photographers — myself included — deliver 20 to 50 sneak-peek images within one to two weeks of the wedding. These are fully edited highlights you can share on social media while you wait for the complete gallery.

### Typical Delivery Timeline

- **Sneak peeks:** 1 to 2 weeks after the wedding (20–50 images)
- **Single-day wedding gallery:** 4 to 6 weeks
- **Multi-day wedding gallery:** 6 to 10 weeks
- **Album design proof:** 2 to 4 weeks after gallery approval
- **Printed album delivery:** 4 to 6 weeks after design approval

## Quality vs Quantity: What Actually Matters

I want to be honest about something in this industry. Some photographers advertise massive photo counts as a selling point. "We deliver 5,000 photos!" That sounds impressive until you realize that 3,000 of them are duplicates, near-duplicates, or images that should have been culled.

A curated gallery of 1,500 stunning images tells your story better than an overwhelming dump of 5,000 unedited frames. Trust me on this. When couples sit down to relive their wedding through their gallery, they want every image to stop them. They want to feel something with each scroll. They do not want to wade through fifteen nearly identical shots of the same moment to find the one great one.

That said, Indian weddings genuinely produce more meaningful moments than most other celebrations. So a higher photo count is natural and expected. The key is that every image in your gallery should be there for a reason — because it captures something beautiful, meaningful, or emotionally significant.

More photos does not mean better coverage. Better moments, better light, and better editing — that is what makes a gallery extraordinary.

## Frequently Asked Questions

How many photos do you take versus how many you deliver?
I typically shoot 3,000 to 5,000 frames per day of coverage. After careful culling and editing, I deliver roughly 25 to 35 percent of those as the final gallery. Every delivered image is individually edited and meets my quality standards.

Can I request more photos from a specific event?
Absolutely. If the sangeet or haldi is particularly important to your family, let me know in advance and I will ensure extra attention and coverage for that event. Communication before the wedding helps me prioritize perfectly.

Do you deliver unedited or RAW files?
I do not deliver unedited RAW files. Every image in your gallery is carefully edited to ensure consistency, quality, and a cohesive visual story. Delivering unedited files would not represent the quality of work I promise to my couples.

How long do I have access to my online gallery?
Your online gallery remains active for a minimum of 12 months. I recommend downloading all your high-resolution images as soon as the gallery is delivered and backing them up in multiple locations for long-term safekeeping.

Will a second photographer increase my photo count?
Yes. A second photographer captures different angles and moments simultaneously, which naturally increases the final gallery size by 30 to 50 percent. For larger Indian weddings, I strongly recommend a second shooter to ensure nothing is missed.
