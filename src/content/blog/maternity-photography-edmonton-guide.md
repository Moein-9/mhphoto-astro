## When to Book Your Maternity Session

Timing is everything with maternity photography. Book too early and your bump may not be visible enough. Book too late and you may be too uncomfortable to enjoy the experience. The sweet spot, based on years of experience, is between 28 and 34 weeks.

### Why 28 to 34 Weeks Works Best

At this stage, your bump is beautifully rounded and prominent. It is clearly a baby bump, not ambiguous. You are still comfortable enough to stand, walk, and pose for 60 to 90 minutes without significant discomfort. Your skin has that pregnancy glow. And you are far enough from your due date that the risk of the baby arriving before the session is minimal.

That said, every pregnancy is different. If you are carrying multiples, your bump will show earlier and you may want to schedule closer to 26 to 28 weeks. If this is your first pregnancy and you are showing later, 32 to 36 weeks might be perfect. I always recommend reaching out to book at least four to six weeks in advance so we have flexibility to adjust based on how your pregnancy progresses.

### First Trimester and Second Trimester Sessions

Some families want to document the entire journey, not just the final stretch. I have done sessions as early as 14 weeks — not to show a bump, but to capture the joy and excitement of early pregnancy. These sessions focus more on the couple's connection, the nursery preparation, and the emotional anticipation rather than the belly itself. They make beautiful additions to a maternity gallery, especially when paired with a third-trimester session.


Photographer Tip

Book your session before the third trimester begins. This gives us a four-to-six-week window to find the perfect date based on weather, your comfort level, and Edmonton's seasonal conditions. Waiting until the last minute creates unnecessary stress during what should be a relaxing, joyful experience.

## What to Wear for Maternity Photos

What you wear to your maternity session has a significant impact on how the photos look and feel. The goal is to celebrate your shape — to wear something that flatters your bump while making you feel confident and beautiful.

### Outfit Options That Photograph Beautifully

- **Fitted maxi dress:** This is the most popular choice for good reason. A long, fitted dress in a solid colour hugs your curves and creates a beautiful silhouette. Flowy fabric at the hem adds movement and elegance.
- **Bodycon dress:** If you want to show off your bump with confidence, a stretchy bodycon dress is stunning. Keep the colour simple — black, cream, or a deep jewel tone — and let your shape be the star.
- **Flowing gown:** For a more editorial or dreamy look, a flowing chiffon or tulle gown creates gorgeous movement in outdoor sessions. The fabric catches wind and light, adding drama to every frame.
- **Casual and authentic:** Jeans and a simple white top. Your partner's oversized shirt. Comfortable clothes that feel like you. Not every maternity session needs to be formal — sometimes the most meaningful photos are the most relaxed.
- **Cultural attire:** Sarees, lehengas, and traditional garments are absolutely stunning for maternity photography. The rich colours, embroidery, and draping create images that honour both the pregnancy and the cultural heritage.

## Best Edmonton Locations for Maternity Photography

Edmonton offers incredible variety for outdoor maternity sessions. Here are my top recommendations based on years of shooting in the city.

### Government House Park

This is one of my favourite maternity locations in Edmonton. The formal gardens offer structure and colour, while the surrounding grounds provide open space and mature trees. It is rarely crowded, which means a more private and relaxed experience. The light in the late afternoon filters through the trees beautifully, creating a golden, warm atmosphere.

### River Valley Trails

Edmonton's river valley is one of the largest urban park systems in North America. The trails offer natural, woodsy backdrops with dappled light filtering through the canopy. For families who want a more adventurous, nature-focused session, the river valley is perfect. The paths are generally accessible for pregnant mothers, with gentle slopes and plenty of rest spots.


### Home Sessions

Some of the most intimate maternity sessions I have photographed have been in the family's own home. The nursery that has been lovingly prepared. The living room where the family gathers. The bedroom where the couple lies together and talks to the baby. Home sessions feel personal and authentic in a way that outdoor sessions cannot always replicate. If your home has good natural light — large windows, light-coloured walls — it can be a beautiful option.

### University of Alberta Campus

The U of A campus offers a mix of architecture and nature. The old buildings provide classic backdrops, while the surrounding gardens and tree-lined paths offer natural settings. It is particularly beautiful in spring when the blossoms are out and in fall when the leaves turn golden.

## Including Your Partner and Children

Maternity photography is not just about the person carrying the baby. It is about the family that is growing. Including your partner and existing children in the session adds depth, emotion, and storytelling to the gallery.

### Partner Involvement

Some partners feel awkward in front of the camera. That is completely normal. My approach is to give gentle direction — stand behind her, place your hands on the belly, rest your chin on her shoulder, whisper something that makes her laugh. The goal is not posed perfection. It is genuine connection. When a partner relaxes into the moment and genuinely engages with their pregnant partner, the photos capture something real and beautiful.

### Including Older Siblings

If you have other children, including them in the maternity session creates some of the sweetest images you will ever own. A toddler kissing the belly. A five-year-old with their ear pressed against the bump, listening for the baby. An older child holding a tiny pair of shoes. These photos become family treasures — proof that the older sibling was part of the story from the very beginning.

With young children, I keep things playful and relaxed. We let them explore, run, pick flowers. The posed shots are quick and intentional. The candid moments — the ones where the child is genuinely interacting with the belly — are the ones that steal the show every time.

## Posing and Comfort During Your Session

Comfort is the number one priority during a maternity session. You are growing a human being. Your back might ache. Your feet might swell. You might need to sit down every ten minutes. And that is completely okay.

I structure maternity sessions with built-in rest breaks. We start with standing poses while energy is highest. We transition to seated or reclining poses as the session progresses. And we finish with relaxed, intimate moments that require minimal physical effort. This arc keeps you comfortable while creating a varied, complete gallery.

### Flattering Poses for Every Body

- **Three-quarter angle:** Stand at a slight angle to the camera rather than straight on. This shows the bump's profile while creating a slimming effect on the overall silhouette.
- **Hands on belly:** One hand on top, one underneath. This classic pose highlights the bump and creates a beautiful frame with your arms.
- **Looking down:** Gaze down at your belly with a soft smile. This creates an intimate moment between you and your baby that translates beautifully in photos.
- **Walking toward camera:** Slow, gentle steps while holding your partner's hand. The movement creates natural body positioning and genuine expressions.
- **Sitting in nature:** Seated on grass, a blanket, or a bench. This removes the pressure of standing and creates a grounded, peaceful composition.

Photographer Tip

Eat a light meal and stay hydrated before your session. Bring water, snacks, and comfortable shoes for walking between locations. If you feel lightheaded or tired at any point, tell me immediately — we will take a break. Your comfort and safety always come first.

## Indoor vs Outdoor Maternity Sessions

Both indoor and outdoor sessions have their strengths. The right choice depends on your personal style, the season, and your comfort level.

**Outdoor sessions** offer natural light, varied backdrops, and a sense of openness. Edmonton's parks, river valley, and urban spaces provide beautiful settings that change with the seasons. Spring offers blossoms. Summer provides lush greens and golden hour warmth. Fall gives you rich, warm tones. Even winter — with snow-covered pines and soft, diffused light — creates stunning maternity images.

**Indoor sessions** — whether in your home or a studio — offer controlled conditions, privacy, and intimacy. You do not need to worry about weather, wind, or passersby. The focus is entirely on you, your partner, and the connection you share. Home sessions, in particular, add a layer of personal meaning because the space itself tells part of your story.

Many families choose to do both — starting outdoors for the scenic variety and finishing indoors for the intimate, close-up work. This combination gives you the best of both worlds and creates a gallery with real diversity and depth.

## Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a maternity session take?
Most maternity sessions last 60 to 90 minutes. This includes time for outfit changes, location moves, and rest breaks. I never rush a maternity session — your comfort and enjoyment are more important than speed.

Can I bring props to the session?
Absolutely. Ultrasound photos, baby shoes, a special blanket, a letter to the baby — meaningful props add personal significance to the images. I recommend keeping props minimal and meaningful rather than numerous and generic.
